To the hiring manager of Atlassian,

This isn't just a cover letter for a design position, it is also a letter of love and admiration. My first encounter with Atlassian software was at the University of Queensland. The subject was DECO2800, a class of 200 students had to collaboratively make a game. Git commands were foreign to me, and the stress of stashing, merging which had the potential hazard of conflicting with others made my head spin. Thats when I discovered Sourcetree and your software lessen the blow, visually making it easier for me to grasp the concept.

My love for the software only grew when I started first UI/UX design job. JIRA and Confluence made communication flow seamless from managers to developers. But it wasn't just the powerful ticketing system that stole my heart. I did some research into Atlassian after attending my first design meetup there. Your no bullshit attitude, empowerment of women in tech and a safe place for learning is what drives me even more to want to be apart of your team.

Now you know why I am here, let me tell you what I would bring to Atlassian.
  1. The desire and drive to learn: from passionate co-workers and be apart of a company that impacts millions of users.
  2. I have natural leadership skills: I am neither just a front end developer nor a UI/UX designer. My background and knowledge range across both spectrums. When I was working with a smaller team I have had to wear many hats and do a bit of everything, providing me with the knowledge of requirements to run a team, projects and software.
  3. Problem Solving: A pursuit to solve real-world problems and help create a positive impact on the world.
  4. I am humble person, an outgoing person and am driven. I will roll up my sleeves, get in there with my humourous jokes (questionable), confidence within myself and team, and love for real world technology.
  5. I am curious: I love asking questions, and a lot of them. I observe, ask questions and seek to understand. Sometimes I don't get it the first go, but I make sure I continue to ask unti I do so there is no confusion.

A bit about me personally, I try to spread positive energy and make people laugh. My interests outside the software world is riding motorbikes around Sydneys hinterland, going to various Meetups and eating patizzis. If you are reading this now, I really want to thank you for your time. It has been a pleasure for me to express my interest to work for Atlassian.

For more information on job history, skills and side projects please explore my portfolio.

Kind Regards,
Madeleine Kingsley
