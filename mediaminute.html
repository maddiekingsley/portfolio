Convergent Production, Social and Mobile Computing (DECO3500)

The course, social mobile and computing brings the use of technology, design and research together to give the chance to create an interactive social invention. Throughout the semester, I was apart of a diverse group of students, placed together to work as a team to imagine and ultimately design a prototype that combined the problem spaces of journalism and IT as one. Journalism students were able to gain a greater understanding of the design process that comes hand in hand with technology and I personally immersed myself within the problem space that journalism is currently facing in this modern day and age as well as improving my research and communication skills.

The inspiration for Media Minute grew from a belief that there exists a huge amount of untapped potential in the realm of digital news consumption. Although our approach was forward-looking and we were committed to making use of modern technology wherever possible, we also looked backwards. We looked back to a time in history where reading the news was simple and didnâ€™t depend on a data connection, full reception or an online subscription.

Problem Space

We envisioned a future where the news was freely available to all of us curious about what was happening to our city, our state, our country and our planet. Despite all our ideas and aspirations, it was clear we had to define the problems we were trying to solve a little more concretely. We did so by identifying three main issues:

1. Access to digital news can be limited
2. Accessing news on mobile devices can cause social isolation
3. News consumption is a private activity, even when it takes place in public spaces

Kickstater Video

Overall Success

At all times during our design process, we worked to ensure that Media Minute did not become a sky-high vision without a purpose. We were committed to developing a system that worked effectively to solve the problems identified earlier in our work. Media Minute is the answer to the issues identified in our problem space because for one, it removes barriers that can arise during digital news consumption. Media Minute is free and available for use by all members of the public. Secondly, It reduces social isolation. Media Minute invites people to put away their mobile devices and interact alongside their fellow citizens. And lastly, it makes news consumption a public activity that occurs in a public space.

Overall, Media Minute was a raging success and on the night of the exhibition won "Best Overall" in competition with 25 other projects.
