Design Computing Studio 3 - Proposal (DECO3800)

A studio-based course in which interdisciplinary teams of students develop a proposal for a significant software-based system. The course aims to consolidate and expand on students previous experience with designing information systems, user interfaces, and computer software and systems. Subjects such as requirements elicitation, business cases, design specifications, and identifying and dealing with professional and ethical issues associated with software projects and products are covered during the course.

UQ Discover Brief

Each year a new wave of students come through the halls of UQ, and it can be an overwhelming experience for many students as they experience new responsibilities and expectations. Because of this, welcoming first year students to campus and encouraging them to meet new people is a large part of UQ orientation week. But despite all the aid, students may not be aware of the more specific resources such as accessibility to submission slots, academic advisor's office, computer access to name a few for a certain facility. For this reason, the client requires a tool to be put forth in order to help first years meet other students, and have information that is directly useful to them and the courses they are taking. T​his tool will assist first year students with feeling comfortable at university and to get to know others in their courses forming friendships through playing the game​.
