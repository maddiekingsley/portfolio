Madeleine Kingsley Resume Projects University Work

Sniip

Android & iOS | Product Designer | Jul 2018 - Dec 2019

About Sniip

Sniip is a FinTech company that has developed a digital payment option where customers can scan a QR or BPAY code to pay their bill. Sniip is a Brisbane based company where I have been from July, 2018 until Decemeber, 2019. Tasks and duties delegated to me expanded the end to end product and customer experience. Starting with discovery and ending with testing and approval of development into production, relying heavily on stories and prototypes in between.

Mission

Our mission is to enable mobile payments where they are currently difficult to make. At Sniip our vision is to take the hassle out of paying bills and use innovative technology to transform paying and managing bills into an experience of effortless efficiency.

What do we do? All payment options in one place. The ability to generate payments from QR codes/ non-tech media. Managing payments i.e bills.

Challenge

Being an in-house tech company, I worked solely on the one product for iOS and Android. To visual the customers journey I conducted 50 interviews to help understand what the journey of a bill would looked like. Starting at how they received their bill, what happens to that that time until payment? What do people do after they pay the bill? And finally what support do they need in wait of the next bill.

Who am I designing for

After conducting interviews, and sending an questionnaire out to active users, we were able to create four personas. Out of the four, between the current market and who our current database mostly related too, we were able to distinguish a particular persona who would be our target demographic. We call this persona a "Life Juggler".

Mainly house owners, they make mental notes about when bills are due so are more susceptible to missing payments. With a majority of volume and value of bills being paid manually, so they are dedicating a time to engage with bill payment, meaning they would be more open to utilising a tool to help facilitate this process will have the money in their account to cover bills.

”If I have the funds I will pay it instantly. If I dont, I pay with Credit Card so I don’t miss it. I have a memory of a goldfish if don’t pay it instantly I forget. I really do struggle haha.” IV.4
“Every Friday I check the pile and then I pay the ones that are due that coming week. They get lost otherwise. I have so many emails! They sometimes get them sent to the bank account, but I forget it’s there. This is a better way for me to organise." IV.17
“Pay on the due date. Cause it my money - I want to hold onto it as long as possible.” IV.33

Research

At Sniip, we worked in 2 week sprints, including the design team. For me, each sprint I was usually tasked with 1-3 stories, depending on the size and complexity.

When I start a story, the first thing I do is research the market. Carefully consider different call to actions, How are other people solving this problem? Are there commonalities? Inspiration is found by looking at what other people are doing. Internal interviews shape the direction and in parallel get feedback early. Using Slack or having a quick conversations, I ask people in the office about their own lived experiences, create polls in channels or send private message with my questions.

Ideation

After the research phase (although that continues throughout) I make use of the classic pencil to paper methodology. There is nothing more satisfying than taking your scattered ideas and thoughts and doing a massive brain dump onto paper. Sometimes the direction I need to take is clear therefore I jump straight into workflows or sketch some basic ideas for the design. Other times I have to collate my thoughts and stew of the different elements of the problem. I ask a lot of internal questions to myself which in turn is usually blurted out to the closest developer to understand my constraints or possibilities. Anyone I can get my hands on is better than being stuck in my own head.

Prototype

Next, the best ideas are selected and then I get my hands dirty in Sketch. Working in two week sprints, which means the faster I can get a design out to show to the team the better (Does fail fast ring a bell?). Roughly mocking something up is usually simple enough due to lot of the reusable components in our design system.

Within this phase, there might be time for 1 or 2 Product Authority Sessions. I make a quick walkthrough within a morning in with then is presented that afternoon to the team. Every week I have the opportunity to showcase designs at any stage of the process. It is my time to use it as I please. Previously, I have used these sessions for content writing, workshops, to present testing results, explore the future vision of the app or show designs from it’s early stages to pixel perfect designs. These sessions have helped me develop a thick skin, my designs are continuously critiqued, yet the team's different perspectives helps get the direction of the app on the path.

Testing

More times than I can count, I have done structured and also very loosely structured interviews with anyone who is willing to give me 10 minutes of their time. Friends, co-workers, professionals, Sniip users and family. Most of the testing I have conducted has consists of usability testing and think aloud walkthroughs. Working in a small company it makes it difficult to organise more formally structured interviews, therefore I work with the resources I have.

Some memorable interviews have stemmed from the support desk. Customers emailing in about an issue or a complaint, in which in turn I have helped than asked if they had further time to discuss the app. Why do they use Sniip? What do they enjoy about the app? And what isn’t? Talking to customers has always proven insightful and a wonderful experience for me.

After having a testing session I usually present my results back so that the team is on the same page. This is usually presented in a presentation, where they can see the tasks and questions then the outcomes.



Refine

RReflecting on all the feedback and I incorporate it back into my designs. Often another showcase of the walkthrough and designs takes place to refine and clarify for development. All final check with others within the business who will be developing the story.

My Role & Challenges

Working at Sniip has definitely challenged me. We had in a design team of two and then in my last 3 months a design team which helped me grow and shape my design process.

A challenge that I faced was the lack of time and resources. Research and user interviews had been completed, but not to the depth that would bring deeper understanding to really solidify why I am doing the things I am doing and for who. What data do I have that backs my claims? Something that I would love to incorporate into the business would be more and sooner. Ask users about future functionality and how they believed it would work or live. This would help lead and explain my designs more confidently if I had more qualitative data to back me up.

I am still learning. The design space has many interesting aspects to it, in which I believe I am still trying to find my speciality. Working at Sniip I was about to see the entire end to end development of the app, dabbled in data analytics and marketing campaigns. The benefit of working in a small company. You see it all.
